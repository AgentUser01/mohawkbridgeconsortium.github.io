Our projects

Sandblast & Paint / Honoré Mercier Bridge Federal Spans

The Honoré Mercier Bridge revitalization project began in 2017 with sandblasting and painting the bridge over Île Maline, starting a journey of sustainability. This on-going work is currently between Piers 37 to 44 and Piers E12 to E15. We are focused on meticulous cleaning and painting of the steel structures, preserving the historical integrity while enhancing the bridge's resilience and aesthetics.

Platform Installation Over the River

MBC installed a rigid modular suspended work platform system spanning the St. Lawrence River from Piers 3A to 10A. This innovative project featured platforms equipped with hot-dipped galvanized steel joists, comprehensive guardrails, and non-perforated plywood floors, ensuring durability and safety. Designed to withstand significant loads while preserving the structural integrity of the bridge, the platforms offered a unique solution that blended technical precision with innovative construction methods. This system not only facilitates efficient operations but was crafted to meet stringent safety standards.

Pier Rehabilitation: MTQ Pier 14A

Successfully completed amid the complexities posed by a fast-flowing river, the repair works on Pier 14A showcased our team's engineering skills and adaptability. To execute the repairs and ensure a dry work area, we constructed a temporary dam around the pier. This adaptability and out-of-the-box thinking allowed us to complete the project safely and effectively. The project not only reinforced the structural integrity of the pier but also demonstrated our commitment to innovative problem-solving and adherence to stringent environmental protection standards.

Bearing Replacement: Pier 2A

To replace the bridge bearings at Pier 2A, MBC had to first install lateral bracing. Once in place, the bridge was lifted and reset on temporary bearings to allow traffic flow during the construction phase. The old bearings were dismantled and removed. New bearings were put in their place and the bridge was again lifted and set to rest on the new bearings. This complex endeavour required strategic planning and precise coordination all while traffic continued overhead. The introduction of advanced lateral restraining systems and the careful handling of the jacking process underscored our commitment to maintaining the highest safety and quality standards throughout the construction phase.

Bearing Replacement: Pier 3A

The reconstruction at Pier 3A stood out as a testament to engineering precision. Not only was the bearings replaced as with Pier 2A, but the entire pier was rehabilitated. Tasked with replacing crucial structural components under the constant vibrations of the roadway above, the project involved intricate maneuvers and the installation of sophisticated jacking beams. Each step was meticulously planned and executed to ensure the stability of the structure, with special measures to manage the environmental challenges of the nearby flowing river. This project not only reinforced the physical infrastructure but also demonstrated our capability to deliver complex solutions in logistically challenging situations.

Deck Replacement: Federal Portion

The deck replacement project on the Honoré Mercier Bridge, initiated in 2008, stands out as a major milestone in Canadian bridge rehabilitation. This extensive initiative was part of a collaborative effort between the governments of Canada, Quebec, and the Mohawk Council of Kahnawake, marking it as the largest bridge rehabilitation project ever undertaken in Canada. The project focused on the complete deck replacement of the entire federal portion of the bridge. This deck replacement ensures its continued service in the highway network system for an additional 75 years.
